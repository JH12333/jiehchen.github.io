About Me

I am Jiehe Chen, majoring in Computer Science. This is my fourth year at PSU. I enjoy coding and 3D modeling in Blender. Among programming languages, I am most familiar with C++, followed by Rust and Python. I started learning 3D modeling before starting college. During summer and winter breaks, I continued developing this skill through online tutorials.

Skills

I am a fast learner and can adapt quickly to new environments. Before starting university, I completed a C# course over the summer in just one week, which gave me a solid foundation. I have experience with several areas of Blender, especially Geometry Nodes, modeling, and sculpting. I also have some experience with material nodes, although I am more confident with Geometry Nodes. Areas I have not yet explored in depth include rendering, rigging and animation, and compositing. Most of my Blender projects focus on procedurally generating assets with Geometry Nodes. For example, the core of my tree generator is based on the Space Colonization Algorithm. Through this project, I significantly improved my problem-solving skills, such as by using built-in field interpolation and element-wise parallelism to reduce the performance overhead caused by repeated nodes.
