About Me

I am Jiehe Chen, majoring in Computer Science. This is my fourth year at PSU. I enjoy coding and 3D modeling in Blender. Among programming languages, I am most familiar with C++, followed by Rust and Python. I started learning 3D modeling before starting college. During summer and winter breaks, I continued developing this skill through online tutorials.

Skills

I possess strong self-learning capabilities and the ability to adapt quickly. During the summer break prior to starting university, I completed a C# course in just one week; this laid a solid foundation for my subsequent computer science coursework. I have developed experience with various modules within Blender—most notably Geometry Nodes, modeling, and sculpting. I also have some familiarity with material nodes, though I am not as confident in this area as I am with Geometry Nodes. Modules I have yet to explore in depth include rendering engines, rigging and animation, and the compositor. My main Blender projects focus on using Geometry Nodes to procedurally generate assets. The core of my tree generator is based on the Space Colonization Algorithm. During this project, I greatly improved my problem-solving skills, for example by using built-in field interpolation and element-wise parallelism to reduce the performance overhead caused by repeat node.
